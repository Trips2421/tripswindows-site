The Pacific Palisades factor

Why Palisades homes need more frequent cleaning.

Pacific Palisades sits where mountains meet ocean, and that geography creates unique challenges for windows. The community is exposed to environmental pressures that flat inland neighborhoods don't face.

Ocean proximity: Salt spray travels miles inland, settling on glass as a fine, persistent film. Oceanview properties see salt buildup in days; even inland homes experience gradual haze accumulation. Canyon dust & wind: Narrow canyons funnel wind, stirring dust and debris from roads, construction sites, and natural terrain. This grit settles on windows faster than in open areas. Fire risk & ash: The 2025 Palisades Fire and ongoing fire season mean ash falls periodically. Ash is corrosive and embeds in frames and seals; it requires specialized removal. Tree canopy: Eucalyptus and sycamore trees are dense throughout Palisades. They shed leaves, seed pods, and fine particles that accumulate on glass and in gutters constantly.

The result: windows that lose clarity faster, gutters that overflow sooner, and frames that corrode if not maintained. That's why we recommend quarterly service at minimum — and monthly for oceanview or canyon-facing homes.

What we recommend
  • Oceanview homes — monthly or every 6 weeks. Salt buildup is relentless and affects view quality dramatically.
  • Canyon and elevated properties — quarterly window cleaning. Wind and dust accumulate faster than flat areas.
  • Post-fire properties — one professional ash removal cleaning, then resume regular schedule. Don't let ash sit in frames.
  • Gutter cleaning — twice yearly minimum, three times yearly if heavy tree coverage. Eucalyptus and sycamore drop debris constantly.
  • Pressure washing — annually for driveways, patios, and exterior surfaces. Canyon dust and soot build up quickly.
  • Screen repair and replacement — as needed. Salt air corrodes screens faster than inland locations.
