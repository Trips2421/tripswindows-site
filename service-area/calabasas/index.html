The Calabasas factor

Why Calabasas homes need regular professional cleaning.

Calabasas sits at the interface between developed communities and the wild Santa Monica Mountains. That geography creates a unique set of challenges for maintaining clean glass and surfaces. Open hillsides, seasonal brush, and undeveloped canyons generate airborne dust and debris that settles on windows constantly — especially during the dry months from May through November.

Santa Ana winds are a major factor. These hot, dry desert winds funnel through the mountain passes and corridors surrounding Calabasas, carrying fine particulate that bonds to glass surfaces. After a Santa Ana event, windows that were clean the day before can look like they haven't been touched in months. The dust isn't just cosmetic — left unaddressed, mineral deposits from wind-blown soil can etch glass over time.

The premium homes in Calabasas are designed around their views and their light. Floor-to-ceiling glass, sliding wall systems, and expansive picture windows are standard features in communities like The Oaks and Park Calabasas. When that glass is dirty, it defeats the purpose of the architecture. Our service keeps the glass transparent and the views unobstructed — which is the entire point of living in Calabasas.

What we recommend
  • Most Calabasas residential homes — quarterly window cleaning. The dry climate and hillside dust make quarterly the baseline for homes in this area.
  • Homes with mountain-facing glass — every 2–3 months. Direct hillside exposure means faster dust accumulation, especially during fire season.
  • Gated community estates (The Oaks, etc.) — quarterly at minimum. These properties have significant investment in appearance and large window counts that benefit from regular attention.
  • Old Town commercial storefronts — biweekly to monthly. The village atmosphere means clean windows are part of the community standard.
  • New construction or post-renovation — one-time deep clean, then quarterly maintenance. Construction dust and film require specialized initial treatment.
  • Pressure washing — 2–3 times per year for residential, annually for commercial. Hillside runoff and dust stain hardscape quickly in Calabasas.
  • Gutter cleaning — twice a year minimum. Oak trees, sycamores, and hillside debris can overwhelm gutters in fall and winter.
