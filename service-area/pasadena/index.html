The Pasadena factor

Why Pasadena homes need regular professional cleaning.

Pasadena sits at the base of the San Gabriel Mountains in a warm inland valley. Unlike coastal neighborhoods where salt spray is the main challenge, Pasadena's climate creates a different — but equally persistent — set of problems for homeowners.

Dust is the biggest factor. Fine particulate from the foothills, construction activity, and the 210 freeway corridor settles on glass year-round and gets baked on during Pasadena's long, hot summers. When Santa Ana winds blow through (typically October through March), they carry even more grit from the mountains and deposit it on every exposed surface.

Then there's the tree canopy. Pasadena is one of the most heavily treed cities in LA County — which is beautiful, but oak pollen in spring, sycamore fuzz in summer, and leaf debris in fall keep windows perpetually coated. Sap drips are common and, if left, can permanently etch glass.

What we recommend
  • Most Pasadena homes — quarterly window cleaning. Keeps ahead of dust, pollen, and seasonal buildup without overdoing it.
  • Homes under heavy tree coverage — every 2–3 months. Sap and pollen accumulate faster and can damage glass if left.
  • Old Town & South Lake businesses — weekly or biweekly exterior glass. Storefronts need to look inviting for foot traffic.
  • Pressure washing — annually for driveways, patios, and walkways. Pasadena's dust and leaf stains build up fast on hardscape.
  • Gutter cleaning — twice a year, especially if you have mature oaks or sycamores. Fall leaf drop and spring pollen can clog gutters quickly.
  • Screen repair — as needed. UV exposure in Pasadena is intense and degrades screen mesh faster than in coastal areas.
