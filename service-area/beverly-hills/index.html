The Beverly Hills factor

Why Beverly Hills homes need regular professional cleaning.

Beverly Hills sits in a basin between the Santa Monica Mountains and the Westside flats — a geography that concentrates dust, pollen, and urban particulate in a way that's tough on exterior surfaces. The hillside properties above Sunset face additional wind exposure that drives grit into glass.

Construction is another constant. Beverly Hills is always building, renovating, or remodeling — and the fine dust generated by nearby projects settles on glass, hardscape, and landscaping throughout the neighborhood. Even properties that aren't under construction feel the effects.

Then there's the standard. Beverly Hills homes are held to a higher aesthetic bar than almost anywhere else. A film of dust that might be tolerable in another neighborhood is immediately noticeable here. That's why the majority of our Beverly Hills clients are on monthly schedules — and the results speak for themselves.

What we recommend
  • Flats estates & large homes — monthly window cleaning. These homes have the most glass and the highest visibility — monthly service keeps them in showroom condition.
  • Trousdale & hillside estates — monthly or quarterly. Elevated exposure means more dust and wind-driven grit on glass. Monthly is ideal; quarterly is the minimum.
  • Rodeo Drive & Golden Triangle businesses — weekly exterior glass. Luxury retail demands impeccable presentation, and we deliver early morning before opening.
  • Pressure washing — annually for motor courts, pool decks, patios, and driveways. Beverly Hills hardscape accumulates dust and leaf stains quickly.
  • Gutter cleaning — twice a year, especially for properties with mature trees. Palm fronds, jacaranda blooms, and ficus debris clog gutters throughout the city.
  • Screen repair — as needed. UV exposure degrades mesh over time, and renovation activity can damage screens on adjacent properties.
