The Brentwood factor

Why Brentwood homes need regular professional cleaning.

Brentwood occupies a unique position — close enough to the coast to catch some marine layer influence, but far enough inland that dust and canyon-driven debris are the dominant challenges. It's a combination that keeps glass dirty year-round if you're not on a schedule.

The canyon areas are the hardest hit. Mandeville and Sullivan Canyons funnel wind-driven dust, pollen, and particulate down from the Santa Monica Mountains. Oak pollen in spring is heavy. Eucalyptus sap drips year-round. And when Santa Ana winds blow through in fall and winter, fine grit from the hills coats every exterior surface in a matter of hours.

Even the flatland neighborhoods along San Vicente and south of Sunset deal with dust from nearby construction, brake dust from Wilshire and the 405 corridor, and seasonal pollen from Brentwood's famous tree canopy. The mature coral trees along San Vicente are beautiful — but they shed debris constantly.

What we recommend
  • Brentwood estates & large homes — quarterly window cleaning at minimum. Homes with extensive glass benefit from monthly service to maintain that pristine look.
  • Canyon homes (Mandeville, Sullivan) — every 2–3 months. Heavy tree coverage means faster pollen, sap, and debris buildup on glass and surfaces.
  • San Vicente businesses — weekly or biweekly exterior glass. Storefront presentation matters on this high-traffic corridor.
  • Pressure washing — annually for driveways, motor courts, pool decks, and patios. Brentwood's leaf debris and dust stain hardscape quickly.
  • Gutter cleaning — twice a year, especially in the canyons. Mature oaks and sycamores drop massive amounts of debris that clog gutters fast.
  • Screen repair — as needed. UV exposure degrades screen mesh over time, and canyon critters can damage screens on lower-level windows.
