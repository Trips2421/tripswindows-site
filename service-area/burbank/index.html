The Burbank factor

Why Burbank homes get dirty faster than you'd think.

Burbank sits in the San Fernando Valley, nestled against the Verdugo Mountains — and that geography creates a unique set of cleaning challenges. The Valley is hotter and drier than the Westside, which means more airborne dust and particulate. Santa Ana winds blow fine grit off the hillsides across the entire city several times a year.

Burbank also has one of the best mature tree canopies in the Valley. That's beautiful for shade and property values, but it means sap, pollen, leaf debris, and small branch litter are constantly settling on windows, rooflines, and gutters. During fire season, ash from brush fires in the surrounding hills adds another layer.

The result: Burbank windows and gutters need more attention than most homeowners expect. Quarterly window cleaning and twice-yearly gutter cleanings keep properties looking their best and prevent the kind of buildup that leads to staining or water damage.

What we recommend
  • Hillside homes — quarterly or bimonthly window cleaning. Large view windows show every speck, and mountain dust is constant.
  • Rancho & Magnolia Park homes — quarterly window cleaning. Heavy tree canopy means sap and pollen on glass year-round.
  • South Burbank homes — quarterly window cleaning. Airport particulate adds to standard Valley dust.
  • Downtown & Media District businesses — weekly or biweekly exterior glass. High-traffic commercial areas need consistent curb appeal.
  • Pressure washing — annually for driveways, patios, and walkways. Valley heat bakes grime into concrete fast.
  • Gutter cleaning — twice a year minimum. Burbank's tree canopy means heavy debris in gutters every season.

Burbank FAQ

Questions about service in Burbank.

How often should I clean my windows in Burbank?

We recommend quarterly for most Burbank homes. The combination of Valley heat, Santa Ana winds, and dust from the Verdugo Mountains means windows accumulate grime faster than you'd expect. Hillside homes with large view windows often benefit from bimonthly service, especially during fire season when ash and particulate are heavier.

Do you clean commercial buildings in Burbank's Media District?

Yes. We service office buildings, production facilities, storefronts, and commercial glass throughout Burbank's Media District and the surrounding business corridors along Olive Avenue and Burbank Boulevard. We offer recurring weekly, biweekly, and monthly commercial schedules and can work around production and business hours.

Can you clean windows on hillside homes in the Burbank Hills?

Absolutely. Many Burbank Hills homes are multi-level with large windows designed to capture valley and mountain views. We bring water-fed poles, extension ladders, and the crew to handle any height safely. We're experienced with the steep driveways and narrow access roads common in the hillside neighborhoods.

Is there a minimum job size for Burbank?

No minimum. Burbank is on our regular east-side routing alongside Glendale, Atwater Village, and Pasadena. There's no travel surcharge — whether it's a small Magnolia Park bungalow or a large hillside estate, we'll take care of it.

Do you offer gutter cleaning in Burbank?

Yes. Burbank has significant tree coverage — especially the mature oaks, sycamores, and ornamental trees throughout the Rancho and Magnolia Park neighborhoods. We recommend gutter cleaning twice a year for homes with heavy tree canopy, and at least once a year for all Burbank properties to prevent overflow and water damage.
