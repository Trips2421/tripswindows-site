What commercial pressure washing actually does for your business.

Commercial pressure washing is the systematic cleaning of every hard outdoor surface your customers, tenants, and employees walk on, drive over, or see from the street. Sidewalks, entryways, parking lots, dumpster pads, loading docks, drive-throughs, and building facades — all of it accumulates oil, gum, food waste, grease, tire marks, and environmental grime that regular sweeping can't touch.

A Trip's Windows commercial pressure wash uses commercial-grade hot and cold water equipment matched to each surface. We adjust PSI by material, pre-treat problem areas with degreasers and detergents, manage all runoff, and work around your business hours so customers never see a hose or hear a machine during peak traffic.

We currently maintain exterior surfaces for brands like Chipotle, Lululemon, Aesop, Starbucks, CVS, UNTUCKit, and West Marine across Los Angeles — many on the same recurring schedule as their window cleaning, coordinated across multiple locations.

What we pressure wash

Every surface your customers see (and the ones they don't).

From the front entrance to the back-of-house. One crew, one contract.

Storefronts & entryways

Sidewalks, entry paths, thresholds, and building facades. The surfaces customers cross first. Gum, stains, and foot-traffic grime removed for a clean first impression every day.

Parking lots & garages

Oil stains, tire marks, leaked fluids, and general grime on concrete and asphalt. Surface cleaners cover large areas efficiently. Drive-through lanes and curbs included.

Dumpster pads & loading docks

Grease, food waste staining, odor-causing buildup, and biohazard concerns. Hot-water wash with degreaser pre-treatment. Critical for health code compliance and pest control.

Building exteriors & facades

Stucco, brick, concrete, and painted walls. Mildew streaks, exhaust staining, and weathering removed with surface-appropriate pressure and detergent. Low-pressure soft wash for delicate finishes.

Drive-throughs & service lanes

High-traffic drive-through lanes, order windows, and pickup areas accumulate food splatter, oil, and tire residue fast. Recurring weekly or biweekly service keeps them clean and code-compliant.

Common questions

Commercial pressure washing — top questions.

The questions we hear most from business owners and property managers. For the full list, see our complete FAQ.

Can you wash before or after business hours?

Yes — most of our commercial pressure washing is done outside of business hours. Early morning (before 7 AM), late evening (after close), and overnight for parking lots and loading docks. Our scheduling window runs from 6 AM to 10 PM, and overnight work can be arranged for large-area jobs.

How do you handle dumpster pad grease and odor?

We pre-treat dumpster pads with commercial-grade degreaser, then hot-water pressure wash at high PSI. This dissolves grease, removes food waste staining, and eliminates the organic buildup that causes odor and attracts pests. For restaurants and food service, we recommend weekly dumpster pad service.

Do you work through ServiceChannel and other FM platforms?

Yes. We're experienced with ServiceChannel, Corrigo, FacilitySource, and other facility management systems. We accept work orders, check in/out, upload completion photos, and close tickets directly in your platform. If you use a system we haven't seen before, we'll learn it at no extra cost.

Can you bundle pressure washing with window cleaning?

Absolutely — many of our commercial clients have a single contract that covers both window cleaning and pressure washing on the same recurring schedule. One crew, one invoice, consistent quality across all exterior maintenance. Bundling typically saves compared to booking the services separately.

What about water runoff and environmental compliance?

We use biodegradable, environmentally compliant detergents and manage all runoff direction. For dumpster pads and areas with grease or chemical pre-treatment, we contain runoff and prevent it from entering storm drains. We're familiar with LA County stormwater regulations and take compliance seriously.
