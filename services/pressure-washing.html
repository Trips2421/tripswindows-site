What pressure washing actually does for your home.

Pressure washing uses a controlled stream of high-pressure water to strip away dirt, grime, algae, mildew, oil stains, and environmental buildup from hard outdoor surfaces. In the LA climate — where marine moisture, eucalyptus pollen, and months of dry dust all take turns on your property — a professional pressure wash restores surfaces that household cleaning can't touch.

A Trip's Windows pressure wash is not a one-size-fits-all blast. We adjust PSI by surface material — lower pressure for painted wood and stucco, higher for unsealed concrete and pavers — so your surfaces get clean without damage. We use commercial-grade equipment with surface cleaners, turbo nozzles, and extension wands matched to the job.

Most Westside homeowners schedule a pressure wash once or twice a year, often pairing it with a window cleaning for a complete property refresh before entertaining, listing, or simply starting the season with a clean slate.

Surface-safe approach

The right pressure for the right surface.

Not all surfaces can handle the same PSI. Blasting stucco at full pressure will pit it. Hitting a wood fence with a zero-degree nozzle will gouge the grain. A lot of DIY pressure washing damage comes from using one setting for everything.

Our crew adjusts PSI, nozzle angle, and distance for every material on your property. Concrete and pavers get 2,500–3,000+ PSI with a surface cleaner for even coverage. Wood fences and decks get 1,200–1,800 PSI with a fan tip. Stucco and painted surfaces get soft-washed at 500–1,000 PSI with a detergent pre-soak.

The result: every surface gets genuinely clean without etching, stripping, or water intrusion.

Common questions

Pressure washing — top questions.

The questions we hear most often. For the full list, see our complete FAQ.

Will pressure washing damage my surfaces?

Not when it's done correctly. We adjust PSI and nozzle type for every surface material — lower pressure and wider fan tips for wood, stucco, and painted surfaces; higher pressure with surface cleaners for sealed concrete and pavers. Damage happens when someone uses one setting for everything. We don't do that.

Can I book pressure washing without window cleaning?

Absolutely. Pressure washing is available as a standalone service. Many clients do pair it with a window cleaning for a full-property refresh, but it's completely optional. We're happy to do just the wash.

How long does a typical pressure wash take?

A standard driveway and patio job takes 1 to 4 hours depending on total surface area and the level of buildup. Full-property jobs that include siding, fences, and walkways can run 4 to 6 hours. Your estimate will include a specific time window.

Is the runoff safe for my landscaping?

We use biodegradable, plant-safe detergents and manage runoff direction to minimize contact with garden beds and landscaping. For pre-treatment chemicals on heavy stains (oil, rust), we contain the area before rinsing. Your plants and lawn are safe.

Do I need to be home during the service?

Not necessarily. Since most pressure washing is done outdoors, we just need access to your exterior and a working outdoor water spigot. Many homeowners leave a gate code and let us handle the rest. If interior access is needed (e.g., to reach a back patio), we'll coordinate that in advance.
