What gutter cleaning actually prevents.

Gutters do one job: move rainwater away from your home's foundation, siding, and roof. When they're clogged with leaves, dirt, eucalyptus needles, palm debris, or shingle grit, that water backs up. It overflows along the roofline, pools around your foundation, saturates exterior walls, and creates conditions for mold, rot, and pest nesting — all of which are far more expensive to fix than a gutter cleaning.

In Los Angeles, gutters tend to get overlooked because it doesn't rain often. But when it does rain, it rains hard. A single November storm can push gallons of water through a clogged gutter system and straight into places it shouldn't be. The time to clean gutters is before the rain comes — not during.

A Trip's Windows gutter cleaning is a full-system service: we clear every linear foot of gutter by hand, flush every downspout to confirm flow, sweep roof edges and valleys that feed debris into the system, and reattach any loose brackets or sections we find along the way.

Why it matters

What happens when gutters stay clogged.

Most gutter damage isn't dramatic — it's gradual. Water that should be flowing through downspouts and away from your home instead overflows at the roofline, sheets down exterior walls, and pools around the foundation. Over months and years, this leads to problems that cost orders of magnitude more than a cleaning.

In coastal LA, the problem compounds. Eucalyptus, palm, and ficus trees shed year-round. Marine moisture accelerates mold growth in packed debris. And because rain is infrequent, homeowners often don't realize their gutters are clogged until the first big storm causes visible damage.

Clogged gutters can cause
  • Foundation erosion — pooling water softens soil around your foundation, leading to settling and cracks.
  • Roof rot & fascia damage — standing water behind the gutter line rots fascia boards and can penetrate roof sheathing.
  • Exterior wall staining — overflowing water leaves permanent mineral and mildew streaks on stucco and siding.
  • Landscape washout — concentrated overflow erodes garden beds and kills plants directly below the overflow point.
  • Pest nesting — packed, moist debris in gutters attracts mosquitoes, birds, rodents, and insects that nest in the organic material.
  • Interior water damage — in severe cases, backed-up water penetrates walls and ceilings, causing mold and structural damage.

How often

How often should you clean your gutters in LA?

Tree coverage and proximity to the coast are the two biggest factors.

Heavy tree coverage
Twice a year

Homes under eucalyptus, pine, ficus, or palm trees. These species shed year-round in LA's climate. Schedule once in late fall before the rainy season and once in spring after pollen season.

Moderate tree coverage
Once a year

Most Westside homes with some tree canopy but not heavy overhang. Annual service in late fall — right before the November-through-March rain window — is the sweet spot.

Minimal trees / flat roof
Every 1–2 years

Homes with little tree exposure still accumulate shingle grit, dust, and wind-blown debris. Even minimal buildup can clog a downspout. Check annually; clean at least every other year.

Common questions

Gutter cleaning — top questions.

The questions we hear most often. For the full list, see our complete FAQ.

When is the best time to clean gutters in LA?

Late fall — ideally October through early November — right before the rainy season begins. This clears out the debris that's accumulated over the dry months so your gutters are ready when the first storms hit. If you have heavy tree coverage, a second cleaning in spring after pollen season is recommended.

Do you clean second- and third-story gutters?

Yes. We routinely service two- and three-story homes using extension ladders with stabilizers and proper safety equipment. There's no upcharge for reasonable height access — it's built into the flat-rate quote.

What happens to the debris?

We bag everything and take it with us. Nothing gets dumped on your lawn, in your garden beds, or on your driveway. The goal is to leave your property cleaner than we found it.

Can I bundle gutter cleaning with window cleaning?

Absolutely — and most customers do. Since we're already on ladders and on-site, adding window cleaning to a gutter visit is efficient and saves you money. One crew, one day, one invoice.

Do you install gutter guards?

We don't install gutter guards, but we can advise on whether they make sense for your home based on what we see during the cleaning. Homes with heavy tree coverage often benefit from guards, while homes with minimal debris may not need the added cost.
